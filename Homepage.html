What Is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to the long-term shift in average weather patterns and temperatures worldwide. While natural factors can affect the Earth's climate, the rapid release of greenhouse gases since the 1800s has led to an average global temperature increase of over 1.5 degrees Celsius.
This has resulting in catastrophic effects such as floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, droughts, and crop failures. Greenhouse gases cause global warming by trapping heat in the atmosphere, as the result of a process called the greenhouse effect.
Sunlight warms the Earth, which then radiates heat back out. Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide prevent this radiation and heat from the sunlight from escaping into space and instead re-radiating some of it back down, which warms the planet.
